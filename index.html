Getting There

The Château is located in the heart of the Ariège Pyrenees, a few minutes drive from Saint-Lizier, a hilltop village, and Saint-Girons, a small town.

The easiest way to get to the area is to fly to Toulouse-Blagnac airport. From there, the options are:

Car/Taxi (approximately 90 minutes).

Public Transport (approximately 3 hours). Please note:

- There is no direct route so you will have to change.

- You will need to use a local taxi service for local travel (see FAQs).

FAQs

Should I bring a gift?

We are not expecting gifts as we appreciate the cost of joining us abroad and your presence is all we ask for.

If you’d still like to gift us something, we’d be very grateful for a contribution towards our honeymoon we’re planning for next year. Japan and New Zealand are top of our bucket list!

You can contribute to our honeymoon fund here.

What should I wear?

We know dress codes can be confusing, so here’s a bit more guidance to help you figure out your outfits!

Friday and Saturday have semi-formal dress codes. In our minds this is somewhere in between casual day-wear and black tie.

Friday

Semi-formal cocktail party.

Theme: Colourful.

Ladies: Long dresses/playsuits. Shoes - comfy as it is generally a standing event.

Gents: Suits or chinos and a blazer, tie optional.

Saturday

Semi-formal garden party.

Theme: Bright/pastel/floral.

Ladies: Dresses/playsuits. Shoes - flats or platforms as we'll be standing on grass/gravel for some of the event. Feel free to bring a pair of shoes to change into for later in the evening!

Gents: Suits, tie optional.

It is likely to be hot during the day and cooler in the evening. Breathable and thin materials for the day, with layers (cardigan/shawl) for the evening is recommended.

Sunday

Casual. Don’t forget your swimsuit!

Will the ceremony and reception be indoors or outdoors?

Weather permitting, the ceremony and reception will be outdoors.

How do I get to and from the Château?

The Château is accessible by car/taxi. Rideshare apps are not available in the local area so you will need to use a local taxi company. We will share details of options closer to the time.

Please note the Château is located on an active country road without pedestrian facilities so walking to the venue is not advised.

Is parking available onsite?

Yes, parking is available at the Château.

What will the weather be like?

The average temperature in September is in the mid-20s, however as the Château is in the mountains the weather is unpredictable. It could be anywhere between 20 - 35 degrees, clear blue sky, chucking it down or anywhere in between. With that in mind please keep an eye on the weather forecast and be prepared for all the above!

Can I bring a plus one?

Unfortunately we cannot accommodate plus ones.

What can I do in the local area?

- Visit a traditional french market in Saint-Girons (Saturday morning only).

- Hike or cycle a local trail or ask the tourist office for more information.

- Take in the beautiful scenery from the viewpoint in Saint-Lizier (in front of le Carré de l’Ange restaurant).

What can I do in the wider area?

- Stone Garden (15 minute drive) is a local river bathing spot.

- Foix (45 minute drive) is a charming medieval town.

- The Sanctuaires Notre-Dame de Lourdes (90 minute drive) is a major Catholic pilgrimage site.

- Les Bains du Couloubret (90 minute drive) is a spa centre with natural thermal waters.
